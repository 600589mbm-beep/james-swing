# ROI Analysis

> **Important:** 2nd Swing's real financials are not public, so every number below is **illustrative** and built from clearly stated assumptions. The point is the *model* and the *order of magnitude*, not a precise forecast. Swap in real figures and the framework still holds. Treat this as a starting point for a conversation, not a guarantee.

---

## Tool #1 — Trade-In Listing Agent

**Where the money is:** every trade-in that arrives has to be inspected, graded, priced, photographed, and written up before it can sell. The writing/pricing/grading-from-notes part is repetitive and AI-assistable.

**Assumptions (edit these):**
- Manual listing time: ~12 min/club (research price + write copy + assign grade)
- With the agent doing a first pass a human approves: ~4 min/club
- Time saved: ~8 min/club
- Volume: 150 clubs/day processed
- Loaded labor cost: $22/hr

**Illustrative annual impact:**
- Hours saved/day = 150 × 8 ÷ 60 = **20 hrs/day**
- Annual (260 working days) = 5,200 hrs → × $22 = **~$114,000/yr in labor**
- Secondary win: faster listing = inventory sells sooner = less cash tied up and fewer price markdowns on aging stock.

---

## Tool #2 — AI Club-Fit Assistant (on-site)

**Where the money is:** most e-commerce visitors leave without buying. An assistant that helps a shopper find the right club lifts conversion — and on a large traffic base, small percentages are large dollars.

**Assumptions (edit these):**
- Monthly site visitors: 500,000
- Baseline conversion: 2.0%
- Average order value: $180
- Assistant lifts conversion by a conservative 0.2 percentage points (2.0% → 2.2%)

**Illustrative annual impact:**
- Extra orders/yr = 500,000 × 0.2% × 12 = 12,000 orders
- × $180 = **~$2.16M in added revenue** (gross; net depends on margin)
- Even a 10x-smaller lift (0.02 pts) is still ~$216K/yr.

---

## Tool #3 — Photo Condition Grader (vision)

**Where the money is:** grading is subjective and slows intake. A vision model that proposes a grade from photos against the published 10-point scale standardizes quality and removes a bottleneck.

**Illustrative levers:**
- Faster intake (compounds Tool #1's savings).
- Fewer grading disputes / returns — even a 0.5-point drop in return rate on a large order base saves real money in restocking and shipping.

---

## Tool #4 — Dynamic Pricing Engine

**Where the money is:** used-club prices move with new releases and seasonality. Repricing by hand can't keep up, so clubs sell too cheap (lost margin) or too dear (dead stock).

**Illustrative levers:**
- A 1–2% margin recovery across a multi-million-dollar inventory is six figures.
- Faster sell-through reduces holding cost and markdown losses.

---

## Tool #5 — Bulk SEO Description Writer

**Where the money is:** thousands of SKUs need unique, search-friendly copy. Templated text ranks poorly; hand-writing it doesn't scale.

**Illustrative levers:**
- Incremental organic traffic is near-zero marginal cost.
- Even a low-single-digit % lift in organic sessions, applied to the conversion + AOV above, adds meaningful revenue.

---

## How to use this in a conversation

Don't lead with the totals — lead with the **model**: "Here's the formula; plug in your real numbers." That signals you think like an owner, not just a coder. The headline: **Tool #1 alone plausibly pays for the role several times over**, and Tool #2 operates on a revenue base where tiny percentages dwarf the salary.
